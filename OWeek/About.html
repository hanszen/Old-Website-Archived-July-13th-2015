What is O-Week?

O-Week takes place the week before classes begin. The only people on campus will be the O-Week staff, you, and your fellow classmates. The O-Week team consists of coordinators (who work so hard all summer to make this week perfect!), advisors, and affiliates—all upperclassmen, who will guide you in your many endeavors throughout the week.

The main purpose of O-Week is academic advising: picking an ideal major (although its okay if you don't have one yet!) and getting you into all the right courses. Not only are the advisors spread out over different majors, but faculty advisors and peer academic advisors will also be here to help you hit the ground running. Don't worry about classes this summer; O-Week will have all the answers! The other goal of O-Week is to introduce you to the Rice experience. Throughout the week, you will learn about all of Rice's unique traditions and also what it means to be a part of a residential college. By the end of the week, you'll bleed blue and yellow, and Harry Clay Hanszen will be your hero. You'll also get to explore Houston and see all of its highlights.

O-Week is unlike any other college orientation, and at times, it may seem a bit overwhelming and a little out of your comfort zone. But never fear, your advisors and coordinators are here! One thing that is great about Rice is the immense amount of support you'll find here, especially at Hanszen (after all, we are the family college). It's important to come into O-week with an open mind and positive attitude… you'll be amazed with what you may learn about yourself! Check out the O-Week book to learn about all things Rice, Hanszen, and O-Week related. Enjoy the rest of your summer and we will see you on August 12th!
