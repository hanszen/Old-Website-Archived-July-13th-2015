Computer Committee Tasks

So what does the computer committee do? Well, in addition to running our Linux server Harry-Clay, we also maintain a network of Macintosh computers for the residents of the college and write web pages for Virtual Hanszen.


Administration of Harry-Clay

Harry-Clay.HanszeNet.Rice.Edu is our college web server. It is a 120-MHz Pentium PC running Linux. This machine gives college members a forum for creating mailing lists and web pages of interest to other college members, accounts with unlimited disk quotas, and constant email addresses for alumni as they leave Rice and get new accounts elsewhere.

Additionally, in maintaining this machine, committee members get valuable experience in Unix system administration, including creation of accounts; filesystem diagnosis and maintenance; management of TCP/IP networking protocols and daemons; and configuration of Domain Name Server (DNS), Network Information Service (NIS), and HyperText Transport Protocol (HTTP) software.


Macintosh Network

The Hanszen college computer lab (affectionately known as the weenie loft), in addition to being an Owlnet lab, contains a network of Macintosh computers. These machines, maintained by the Hanszen College Computer Committee, provide college members with access to productivity software such as Word and Excel for working on term papers and reports.


Consulting

In addition to maintaining computers owned by the college, the Computer Committee members help college residents to set up their network operating systems--including Windows for Workgroups, Windows 95, Windows NT, and MacOS--and answer questions about getting machines connected to the dorm Ethernet network.

Also, Computer Committee members have frequently provided assistance on an ad-hoc basis to computer-oriented projects around the college and the university, including Student Association online officer voting, configuration of an email express terminal in the college coordinator's office, and the installation of air-conditioning in the weenie loft.
