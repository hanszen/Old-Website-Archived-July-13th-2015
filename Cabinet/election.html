Hanszen College Election Code
Hanszen College Election Code

ARTICLE I. Elections

The Elections Justice shall provide for the following elections:

Section A. Fall Election, in September

Section B. Officer Election, in February

Section C. Spring Election,
no less than one (1) and no more than two (2) weeks after the Officer Election, exclusive of University holidays. During this election, nominations will be made for the Hanszen awards, as defined in the Bylaws.

Section D. Special Elections.

  1. For any purpose designated by the Constitution or Bylaws. Elections to fill vacant positions are regulated in the Constitution under Article IV, Section C, Number 6.
  2. For any other purpose, upon the request of the Cabinet and at the leisure of the Elections Justice.


ARTICLE II. Notification of Elections

Section A. Scheduling.
The Cabinet and the Elections Justice shall set the date of elections.

Section B. Notice.
The Elections Justice shall give notice of elections in the minutes of Cabinet meetings and by dinner announcements. Notice shall be given in advance of the elections according to the following guidelines:

  1. Elections and recall elections: at least two (2) weeks in advance.
  2. Elections involving substantive issues: at least one (1) week in advance.
  3. Runoff elections: when the election results are announced. A runoff should be held three (3) to seven (7) days after the first election, and no write-in votes will be accepted.


ARTICLE III. Candidates

Section A. Eligibility.
Each candidate or officeholder must be a member of the College and not on University disciplinary or academic probation. The Elections Justice shall determine whether each candidate fulfills all requirements set forth in the Constitution and this Election Code.

Section B. Declaration of Candidacy.
To become a candidate for elective College positions, a College member shall file with the Elections Justice, by the fifth (5th) calendar day preceding the election, a petition of twenty-five (25) names for College Officer, or of ten (10) names for Section Representative, together with a signed statement that she has read and understands the Constitution, Bylaws, Election Code, and Judicial Code of Hanszen College. Names on the petition shall be those of members of her constituency. Due to the potentially large number of students represented, it shall be permissible for a member of the College to sign more than one petition.

Section C. Write-In Candidates.
The right to cast a write-in vote in any College election (except a runoff) shall be preserved. If, after the election, a write-in candidate is deemed eligible to hold the office for which she was written in, she shall then be declared the winner or qualified for a runoff election as appropriate.


ARTICLE IV. Campaigning

The rules governing campaigning shall be:

Section A.
No posters or signs shall be used for campaign purposes.

Section B.
No campaign materials relating to candidates may be placed in the College mailboxes, nor shall any form of electronic mail be used for campaign purposes.

Section C.
No money shall be spent for campaign purposes.

Section D.
Candidates for the offices of President, Internal and External Vice-Presidents, Secretary(ies), Treasurer, Chief Justice, and Social Coordinator(s) shall be given the opportunity to speak at a College meeting during the week preceding the election.


ARTICLE V. Poll and Vote-Counting Regulations

Section A. Poll Hours
Polls shall be open in the commons from at least 11:45 A.M. until 12:40 P.M., and from 5:30 P.M. until 7:00 P.M. on the day of the election. Additional polls may be opened at different locations at the discretion of the Cabinet and the Elections Justice.

Section B. Conflict of Interest
A candidate shall be ineligible to sit at the polls or to count the ballots on which her name appears.

Section C. Supervision
At least three (3) people shall be present when the votes are counted.


ARTICLE VI. Voting Method

Section A. Ballots
All voting shall be done by secret ballot. The right of the absentee ballot shall be preserved.

Section B. Tallying
Votes shall be tallied using the following system:

  1. Each voter shall be asked to mark her choice of candidates on the ballot, including a write-in candidate if she so chooses.
  2. In an election for one (1) office (the Officer Elections):
    1. The ballots cast for each candidate shall be counted.
    2. If one candidate has the majority of votes cast, she shall then be declared the winner.
    3. If no candidate has a majority, then a runoff will be declared between the two (2) candidates with the highest number of votes. The candidate who garners a majority of votes in the runoff shall then be declared the winner.
    4. If two (2) candidates, for reason of a tie or discrepancy of ballots, cannot be identified for the runoff, all candidates affected by the tie or discrepancy shall be included in the runoff. After the runoff, the candidate with a plurality shall then be declared the winner. For example, if the second (2nd) and third (3rd) place candidates are tied, the top three (3) candidates would be placed in the runoff.
  3. In an election for Section Representatives:
    1. Each voter shall have the opportunity to cast a number of votes equal to the number of positions available.
    2. The ballots cast for each candidate shall be counted.
    3. If any candidate has a majority of votes, she shall be declared the winner.
    4. If the number of candidates achieving a majority is less than the number of positions available, a runoff election will be held with the number of candidates equal to the number of available positions plus one (1). Candidates with the highest number of votes among those who did not receive a majority in the first election will be declared candidates in the runoff. The candidates who garner a plurality of votes in the runoff shall be declared the winners.
    5. If two (2) or more candidates, for reason of a tie or discrepancy of ballots, cannot be identified for the runoff, all candidates affected by the tie or discrepancy shall be included in the runoff. The candidates with pluralities in the runoff shall then be declared the winners. For example, if in Section One, one (1) candidate receives a majority, but the third (3rd) and fourth (4th) place candidates are tied, than the second (2nd), third (3rd), and fourth (4th) place candidates would be placed in the runoff.


ARTICLE VII. Validation and Approval of Elections

If no procedural errors justify invalidation of the election, the Judicial Committee shall receive challenges to the election within three (3) days after results are posted. If a challenge is received, the Judicial Committee shall then meet to approve or disapprove the election. Ballots shall be saved until the election is approved. When the election is approved, the winners shall be declared.


ARTICLE VIII. Turnover

Section A. Officers.
Officers shall take office no more than three (3) weeks after the Officer Election.

Section B. Section Representatives.
Section Representatives shall take office no more than two (2) weeks after the Spring and Fall Elections.


ARTICLE IX. Amending the Election Code

Section A. By the Cabinet.
The Election Code may be amended by a three-fourths (3/4) vote of the Cabinet, but only with the concurrence of the Chief Justice and the Elections Justice.

Section B. By the College.
The Election Code may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the College, provided that at least fifty percent (50%) of the total College membership votes in the election.
