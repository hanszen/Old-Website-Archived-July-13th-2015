Coordinators for Hanszen O - Week 1997

Trey Hamilton - trey@rice.edu

Hanszen '99

Hey there brothers and sisters! I'm a junior mechanical engineering major <mek - ee> from the great megalopolis of Greenville, Alabama... the state that's first in the alphabet and last in adult literacy. I'm here to make sure that your transition to Rice is as smooth as a baby's bare bottom, so with that in mind, here are a few tips to help you out over the next four or five years:

The Allman Brothers Band is the greatest thing that ever happened to music. Always mind your Ps & Qs. John Belushi is the funniest man to ever live... John Cusack and Mike Myers are the funniest men alive. Going to bed before 3 AM is bad for your health. If it tastes good, it must be good for you. A letter from a friend is better than a paycheck anyday. Black Stingray convertibles from the 70s are the greatest cars ever made. Never lie. Elvis is alive, and he works at the Burger King on the corner of Main and Holcombe... he gives me free cheeseburgers. No one ever graduates from college and says, "I wish I had spent more time studying." Watch reruns of The Wonder Years. Wood pencils are better than mechanical ones. You should always eat barbecue on the Fourth of July. I am not Tiger Woods. You haven't lived until you've craved IHOP pancakes at 4 AM... and then gone with your roommate because he has the same craving... and then seen ten more Rice students there. Parties aren't the only way to have fun. The Mighty Morphin Power Rangers wish they were as cool as the Transformers. Michael Jordan isn't worth a thousand public school teachers. Goonies never say die. It's fun to play musical instruments even if you don't know how. Be sure to spend some quality time with your toilet. Every trip you take, short or long, should have a story to go with it. Say "yes, ma'am" and "yes, sir." HFH! "Big wheels keep on turnin', carry me home to see my kin..."

Dave Murgatroyd - dmurga@rice.edu

Hanszen '99

It wasn't Fred Flintstone, but his fellow Hanna-Barbera character Snaggle Puss who said "Heavens to Murgatroyd." Well, congratulations, you've found the person he was talking about!

Born at an early age one David Keith Murgatroyd (you can call me Dave), I spent my formative years in the suburbs of Chicago, but soon (twelve years later) found my way to Bedford, Texas, between Fort Worth and Dallas, where I remained until coming to Rice. Along the way, I developed an affinity for Computers and Applied Math, along with most other branches of knowledge. As a result, I am majoring in Computer Science and Computational and Applied Mathematics here at Rice, now entering my third year. I rarely, however, let my academics get in the way of why we're really here: to have fun! (parents, read: to learn!) This usually occurs while I'm spending time with friends, be they hanging out at Hanszen, serving on Baptist Student Ministry Coordinating Council, leading tours, skiing, traveling, playing chess, serving on Hanszen and University Committees, or collecting US Presidential Memorabilia (yes, really).
