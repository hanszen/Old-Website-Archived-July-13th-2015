    O-Week

    Welcome to Rice University, and welcome to Hanszen College! As the Hanszen Orientation Week Coordinators, it truly is our distinct pleasure to be some of the first people to welcome and congratulate you on not only the things you have accomplished, but the things you will accomplish as well. We are so excited to welcome you into the Hanszen Family, and we can’t wait to get to welcome you and your classmates in person on August 16th.

    Before we get started let us introduce ourselves. Our names are Solji, Natalie, and Trenton. We are current undergraduates (all three of us will be juniors this fall) who were selected last December to plan, organize, and carry out your upcoming week-long orientation and welcome to Rice University. But your orientation does not begin and end in a week. Our job is you help ease everybody’s transition to Rice, both academically and socially. Rice will be a new environment for you, and we have worked to find the best people, programs, and events to make this transition as smooth and successful as possible.

    Throughout orientation week (or “O-Week”, as you’ll hear most Rice students call it) you  will meet hundreds of other new students, as well as returning undergraduate students, faculty, and staff who will become resources and friends for you throughout your time at Rice. The amount of information you receive during O-Week can be overwhelming, so we have made this book to help give you any information that we can give you beforehand so you can begin to learn and understand what it really is to be a Rice student. We have tried to make this book a reliable source of information (at least more reliable than facebook or random internet forums) on everything that we think it could be useful to know (or at least have access to). To that end, we have divided this book into relevant sections that you can look through. Whether you read this book cover to cover, or flip through it and just check out the pictures, we hope this book gives you some insight into Rice.

    This book is one of over a thousand that other new students are receiving now as well (although the books for students at other residential colleges receive a different version). Each residential college not only has its own book, but also its own O-Week theme as well. For Hanszen O-Week 2015, the theme is Hawaii Five O-Week. This theme was chosen because of its ties to Hawaii and Hawaiian culture. Hanszen is considered “the Family College,” and, to quote Lilo and Stitch, “Ohana means family, and family means no one gets left behind,” which captures the culture of Hanszen College.

    But the Family College is more than an idea. It is an identity that we have made for ourselves. At Hanszen, you will find a support system of fellow students, our adult team of faculty and staff that live and work at Hanszen, and other community members that are all around to help you out with any problem you may have. As a family, we work to lift our fellow Hanszenites up when they are down, praise them when they are successful, and are never short on compliments. But the most important part of the family is knowing that every student is unique, and that we want to help you make your Rice experience exactly what you want it to be. No two people have the same Rice experience, and we want to provide you with the knowledge and capabilities to blaze your own trail and explore your own horizons. Our job is not to tell you what to do at Rice, but to help you discover what you want to do with your time at Rice. That is what we have spent this summer working on.

    We’re sure you have tons of questions, so please send us an email so we can help you figure out everything you can (our contact info is on the next page). Now relax, and enjoy your last summer before college. Your friends may be going to shorter orientations and registering for classes throughout the summer, but we will take care of all of that during O-Week this August. Trust us when we say that all your questions will be answered when you get here, and we could not be more excited to finally meet you!

     

    - Solji, Natalie, and Trenton
