Set in 1940's Holloywood, City of Angels portrays two seperate but overlapping story lines. One story is of a successful crime writer from New York, Stine. We find Stine out of his element in glamorous Hollywood attempting to successfuly adapt one of his best-selling thrillers into a movie. The other story, a 1940's detective movie, revolves around Stine's main character, Stone, a private eye.

Scene by scene, the two stories intertwine as the real life of Stine is played in vivid color and his movie is portrayed in the blacks, whites, and grays of classic film noir style. The collisions between reality and fiction combine with a jazzy musical score create a smart, witty and powerful show that is in fact a revealing examination of the gray areas between life and fantasy.

City of Angels won six Tony Awards in 1990 including Best Musical and Best Musical Score.
